# 10 Smart Weight Loss Foods to Incorporate in Your Diet ...

When it comes to **smart weight loss foods**, the year 2024 is bringing in some exciting trends and delicious options that can make your journey to a healthier you both enjoyable and effective. Now, before you roll your eyes thinking “Oh great, another list of foods that taste like cardboard,” let me assure you, these are anything but boring. Picture this: juicy fruits, crunchy veggies, and even some indulgent treats that will have you wondering how they can possibly be good for you.

Kicking off with our first **primary keyword**, smart weight loss foods are not just about cutting calories but are more about making actual **healthier choices**. These foods are packed with nutrients, fiber, and are generally lower in _sugar_ and unhealthy fats, meaning they keep you fuller for longer and boost your metabolism without dragging you into a food coma.

Speaking of fiber, one of my personal favorite smart weight loss foods has got to be _avocados_. Yes, the beloved guac-makers are a powerhouse, full of healthy monounsaturated fats that not only help with slimming down but also keep your skin glowing. If you’ve ever felt envious of those avocado toast-loving millennials (I know I have), now’s your time to shine!

Moving on to another yummy option, let's talk about berries. Whether you’re adding them to your morning oatmeal or blending them into a smoothie, berries are low in calories but pack a punch with antioxidants and vitamins. _Blueberries_, in particular, are known to improve overall _health_ while giving you that sweet fix without any of the guilt.

Now, I have to share a funny story about _Greek yogurt_. When I first started my weight loss journey, I couldn’t understand why people raved about it until I realized I was buying the wrong kind. Once I found the unsweetened version and dressed it up with some honey and nuts, it quickly became a staple in my diet. It’s high in protein, helping with muscle repair after those grueling workout sessions you’ve been promising yourself you’d start.

To make sure you’re fully prepped, we’ve included a comprehensive list of these [superfoods](#4) you should be adding to your shopping list. From **green leafy veggies** like _kale_ and _spinach_, to nutrient-dense grains like **quinoa** and **brown rice**, we've got you covered.

So, ready to dive into the world of delicious and smart weight loss foods? You'll wonder why you didn't start sooner. Here's to a healthier, happier you in 2024!

## 1. Apples

Apples do more than keep the doctor away. Lots of fruits make great [weight loss foods](https://food.allwomenstalk.com/simple-and-healthy-foods-to-help-you-lose-weight-while-dieting/), but apples are particularly beneficial. They're full of antioxidants, and according to Self.com they can even help you lose an overall “apple body” (as opposed to an “apple bottom,” mind). Eating them can really help boost your metabolism, so the next time you're looking for a snack, make sure you consider any variation of this popular fruit. Include it in other recipes too, like tuna or chicken salad – or your next garden salad, for that matter!

[self.com](https://www.self.com/gallery/20-superfoods-slideshow)

## 2. Oats

You have to choose foods for weight loss that include all the nutrients, vitamins, and other essentials that your body needs. That includes fiber, which is important not just for your daily dietary needs but also for digestion. Oats are a fantastic source of fiber, and they're incredibly filling. Not only can you eat oatmeal for breakfast, you can use it in healthful snacks, like cookies and granola bars. Use it in turkey meatloaf or in other recipes where you need a filler but prefer to use something other than bread or crackers. See, oats are filled with good carbs, the kind that help your body burn fat while improving your metabolism.

## 3. Kale

If you think kale is just a garnish or literal rabbit food, think again! It's low in calories but high in fiber, plus a small serving is able to provide you with tons of calcium and iron. You can include it in salads for a wonderful, slightly tangy, earthy flavor. It also mixes well with stir fries, soups, and sauteed veggies. Eat it raw or cooked, either way it packs a huge punch and you'll notice the benefits quite quickly.

## 4. Fish

It's a well known fact that fish of all types are good for you. Salmon is the most talked about fish, and I'm not mad at it for that, because it really is good for you. It's good for your heart and it's ideal for weight loss too, thanks to all those fatty acids. However, believe it or not, sardines are really good for you as well. They've got those important omega-3 fatty acids too, not to mention a ton of protein and calcium. If you need a snack, try some on crackers, or use them in place of anchovies. Self.com even has a trick for getting rid of that overwhelming fishiness: just soak your sardines in milk!

[self.com](https://www.self.com/gallery/20-superfoods-slideshow)

## 5. Beef

Beef has a really bad reputation, but it's actually one of the [best weight loss foods](https://food.allwomenstalk.com/foods-perfect-for-weight-loss/) around. Sure, you can't eat it all the time, you should stick to lean cuts, and you shouldn't eat huge amounts of steak, but a little bit is great. Eat it on your salads, try some sirloin, or put it in stir fry or healthy fajitas. Just don't gorge yourself on hamburgers or eat it every day of the week and you'll be fine. Plus, if you can go organic, that's extremely helpful as well.

## 6. Beans

Beans, beans, the musical protein, the more you eat, the more you increase your chances of getting lean! Okay, that's a horrible rhythm for a rhyme scheme, but you get what I mean! Beans are so good for you, because they're a great way to get protein. If you eat a mere cup of yummy black beans, you'll get a ton, but you won't have to worry about the saturated fat. Red beans and white beans are also good for you when you're trying to lose weight, since they're packed with fiber and Resistant Fiber, which is actually a beneficial carb!

## 7. Eggs

Eggs have a bit of a bad rap too, or at least they did a few years ago. The truth is that if you're looking for a yummy weight loss food for breakfast, lunch, or even dinner, an egg is your best friend. Stay away from fried eggs, but otherwise, feel free to eat them boiled, scrambled, poached, or made into omelets and frittatas. However you like them, they've got lots of protein and they'll keep you full for longer amounts of time. Yay eggs!

## 8. Broccoli & Cauliflower

Naturally, almost all vegetables are good for you, but these two are big names in veggie land. I'm putting them together, honestly, because they've always looked alike to me! Cauliflower has lots of vitamin C, and there are strong suggestions that it can help prevent and fight cancer. It's got lots of fiber, plus it tastes great raw or cooked. Broccoli is beneficial for the same reasons, which may be why the two of them are a match made in heaven.

_health.com_

## 9. Grapefruit

In the morning for breakfast or for snack times, grapefruits are super weight loss foods. Health.com advises you to eat half a grapefruit before every meal you eat during the day, because doing so can help you lose weight. In fact, you can shed as much as a pound a week! Grapefruits also have lots of vitamin C, plus a surprising amount of protein. It also helps keep you fuller for longer, so you're not so tempted to snack on unhealthy foods.

_health.com_

## 10. Avocados

Last but certainly not least, an avocado makes a fantastic weight loss food when you're trying to eat right and shed some pounds. Mashed or sliced, avocados make a great substitute for mayo or Miracle Whip, and it contains all the right fats. You can't eat too much, but a little bit definitely goes a long way, so incorporate it into salads, sandwiches, wraps, and snacks.

There are so many incredibly delicious weight loss foods, from fruits and vegetables to lean meats and wonderful proteins. It's never been easier to start eating healthy, you just have to have the will and the motivation to do it! Even if you just start incorporating one or two healthier meals into your diet each week, you'll start to see a difference, not just in terms of weight loss but also in terms of your overall health. What are some of your favorite foods for weight loss?
