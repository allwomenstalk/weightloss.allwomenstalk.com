# Some Important Things to Think about before Getting Weight Loss Surgery ...

There are many things to consider before getting weight loss surgery. After all, a procedure like this can change your life forever. Many people also see weight loss surgery as their final attempt to get their weight under control. However, it’s important to realize that this type of procedure isn’t right for everyone. So, before you schedule an appointment with your doctor, be sure to take a look at this list of 7 things to consider before getting weight loss surgery.

## 1. Cost

One of the many things to consider before getting weight loss surgery is the cost of this type of procedure. The cost of weight loss surgery does vary, but is usually around $25,000. This estimate does not include the cost of follow up appointments or the cost of lost wages. Some insurance companies will cover part or all of the cost of weight loss surgery. If your insurance provider won't pay for this type of procedure, many doctors offer payment plans that you may be able to take advantage of.

## 2. Weight Loss Programs

Have you considered joining a weight loss program? Many doctors feel that their patients should try to lose weight by changing their diet before they consider having weight loss surgery. Some people have had a lot success with programs like Weight Watchers and Jenny Craig. If these types of programs don’t work for you, consider speaking with a nutritionist. They may be able to customize an eating plan that will work well for you.

## 3. Are You a Candidate?

Regardless of what some people believe, not everyone qualifies for weight loss surgery. In order to qualify for weight loss surgery, you normally have to have a body mass index (BMI) of 40 or above. If you have health problems like heart disease or diabetes, you may qualify for weight loss surgery if your BMI is over 35. Most of the people who have weight loss surgery are at least 80 pounds overweight.

## 4. Complications

Just like with many other procedures, complications can occur with weight loss surgery. After having weight loss surgery, it is possible to develop things like blood clots, ulcers or gallstones. When you have weight loss surgery, it is also possible for you to develop an infection.

## 5. Recovery

The recovery time for weight loss surgery does vary by person and depends on the type of surgery that you have done. Some people are able to recover in about a week. Others find that it takes them months to return to their normal activities. After weight loss surgery, some people are in a great deal of pain. Also, as part of the recovery process, you will also need to have several follow-up appointments with your doctor.

## 6. Not a Quick Fix

Before you have weight loss surgery, it's important to realize that a procedure like this is not a quick fix. You must be willing to make lifestyle changes. Also, if you tend to overeat when you become sad or angry, weight loss surgery will not prevent emotional eating.

## 7. Going to a Support Group

Whether you're still considering your options or if you've already had weight loss surgery, going to a weight loss support group may be a good option. It will allow you to ask questions that you have. You will also have the support of others who are in a similar situation. It will also give you a chance to share your experience with others.

As you can see, there are many things that you should consider before you have weight loss surgery. Have you or someone you know had weight loss surgery? What was the experience like?
